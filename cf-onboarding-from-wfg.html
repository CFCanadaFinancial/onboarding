Your license is yours; it moves with you. What changes is the structure around it: from a captive hierarchy to an independent MGA where your clients are your clients and your compensation is transparent. Here's how the move is done cleanly.

A confidential conversation

Day 1

Before any paperwork: a private call with CF leadership. We map your current situation — license status, production, your WFG hierarchy position — and confirm the move makes sense for you. Nothing is communicated to anyone until you decide.

Book a confidential discovery call.

2

Review your WFG agreement

Day 1–5

Your existing agreement may contain non-solicitation or other post-departure obligations. Understanding them before you move is what makes a transition clean instead of contentious.

Locate your signed WFG associate agreement.
Review its non-solicitation and confidentiality clauses.
Escalation point: CF coordinates the transition process, but interpreting your contractual obligations is a legal question. If your agreement contains restrictive clauses, we'll recommend you get independent legal advice — we will never guess at this on your behalf.
3

Verify license & E&O standing

Day 3–7

Your provincial license and continuing education standing transfer with you — no re-exam. We confirm everything is current so nothing surprises us mid-transfer.

Confirm your license is active and CE credits are current.
Confirm your E&O coverage and whether it's individual or provided through your current structure — if it lapses at departure, we arrange replacement coverage before you resign.
4

Prepare CF contracting in parallel

Week 1–2

We prepare your CF Canada Financial contracting package and Apexa updates before your departure date, so your out-of-market gap — the days you can't write new business — is as close to zero as possible.

Complete the CF contracting package and advisor screening checklist.
Update your Apexa profile to reflect the pending transfer (Elena guides the exact sequence and timing).
5

Resign from WFG

Your chosen date

A short, professional, written notice. No drama, no burned bridges — the industry is small and your reputation travels with you.

Submit written notice per your agreement's requirements.
Keep a dated copy of everything you send and receive.
Return any WFG property and complete their exit requirements promptly.
6

Carrier transfers

Week 2–3

Each carrier you're appointed with processes a transfer of your contracting to CF Canada Financial. Some carriers require a release or non-objection from your former organization; others process on notice. We manage each carrier's process for you.

Give Elena your full list of carrier appointments and advisor codes.
Sign the transfer/appointment forms we prepare per carrier.
Track each carrier's confirmation — Elena reports status weekly until all are through.
Good to know: carrier release policies differ and change. Elena confirms the current requirement per carrier for your file rather than relying on what was true last year.
7

Client communication — done compliantly

Week 2–4

What you may say, to whom, and when depends on your former agreement and privacy rules. We help you build a communication plan that protects you while keeping the relationships you've earned.

Build your client communication plan with CF's guidance (and legal advice where clauses require it).
Update your professional profiles — LinkedIn, email signature, voicemail — on the agreed date.

Complete CF orientation and platform access setup.

Plain-language glossary

Captive vs. independent
A captive advisor sells within one organization's structure; an independent advisor contracts through an MGA with access to the full market.
Non-solicitation
A contract clause limiting who you may approach after leaving. Interpretation belongs to a lawyer, not a checklist.
Out-of-market gap
Days between leaving one organization and being contracted at the next, when you can't submit new business. Our job is to minimize it.
Carrier release
Some insurers require your former organization's sign-off before transferring your appointment.

A note on accuracy

Contractual and legal questions about your departure are referred to independent legal advice. Licensing and carrier requirements are confirmed by CF staff for your specific file.
