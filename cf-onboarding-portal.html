The Fresh Start — from LLQP to your first application.

You've done the hard academic part. What remains is a sequence of administrative steps that trip up new advisors only when nobody explains the order. Here is the order.

StepsEight
Typical duration4–8 weeks
Your coordinatorElena's team

1

Confirm your LLQP completion

Day 1

Your LLQP certification is the entry ticket. We verify all modules are complete and your certification window is still valid before anything else moves.

Send Elena your LLQP certification of completion (all modules, including provincial exam eligibility).
Confirm which province(s) you intend to be licensed in — for most of our advisors, that starts with British Columbia.
Good to know: LLQP certification has an expiry window before you must sit the provincial exam. Your CF coordinator will confirm the current rule for your province — we never let a certification quietly lapse.
2

Pass the provincial licensing exam

Week 1–2

In BC, licensing runs through BCFSA. Book the exam early; seat availability is the most common source of delay at this stage.

Book your provincial exam sitting as soon as your LLQP is confirmed.
Tell Elena your exam date — we schedule the rest of your onboarding around it.
Send your pass confirmation the day you receive it.
3

Apply for your license

Week 2–3

Your application to the regulator includes background checks and, in BC, a sponsorship/recommendation component. CF Canada Financial supports you through this — it's a signal to the regulator that a supervising organization stands behind you.

Complete the regulator's application (Elena sends you the current link and a walkthrough).
Complete the criminal record check the regulator requires.
Pay the application fee and forward your confirmation.
Escalation point: anything unusual in your history (prior discipline, bankruptcy, criminal record) is not a dead end — but it must be disclosed correctly. Flag it to Elena immediately and licensed CF staff will guide the disclosure. Never guess.
4

Secure your E&O insurance

Week 2–3 (parallel)

E&O coverage is mandatory before you can act on your license. This runs in parallel with your license application so it's never the bottleneck.

Ask Elena for CF's preferred E&O providers and any group-rate options available to CF advisors.
Bind coverage and send your certificate of insurance to Elena.
5

Create your Apexa profile

Week 3–4

Apexa is the single profile Canadian carriers and MGAs use to screen and contract you. Fill it once, fill it right, and every carrier appointment afterwards moves faster.

Accept the Apexa invitation Elena sends you.
Complete every section: identity, license, E&O, employment history, disclosures.
Upload supporting documents — Elena gives you the exact list so nothing bounces back.
Good to know: incomplete Apexa profiles are the #1 cause of contracting delay industry-wide. We review yours before submission.
6

Sign your CF Canada contracting package

Week 4

Your agreement with CF Canada Financial: compensation structure, compliance obligations, and the advisor screening checklist required by our regulator. Delivered for e-signature — most advisors complete it in one sitting.

Review the contracting package (Elena walks you through any clause on request).
Complete the advisor screening checklist honestly and fully.
E-sign and return.
7

Carrier appointments

Week 4–6

We initiate your appointments with the carriers you'll write for — Manulife, Canada Life, Equitable, iA, and others as your practice requires. Each carrier reviews your Apexa profile and issues its own advisor code.

Confirm with Elena which carriers to appoint first (start focused; add carriers as your practice grows).
Respond same-day to any carrier follow-up requests — speed here compounds.
Record each carrier code as it arrives.
8

Ready-to-write review

Week 6–8

The final gate: a working session with your CF team to confirm illustration software access, application workflows, and compliance basics — so your first application is clean, compliant, and confident.

Complete your new-advisor orientation with the CF team.
Get access to carrier illustration tools and CF's advisor resources (including the CF-ABC Business Building Course).
Book your first joint-work or case-review session — you never write your first case alone.
The milestone: when this step closes, you are contracted, appointed, insured, and ready to submit your first application. That's the whole point.

From WFG to independence — discreetly, and in order.

Your license is yours; it moves with you. What changes is the structure around it: from a captive hierarchy to an independent MGA where your clients are your clients and your compensation is transparent. Here's how the move is done cleanly.

StepsEight
Typical duration2–4 weeks
ConfidentialityAbsolute
Your progress — tap a number to mark a step complete0%
1

A confidential conversation

Day 1

Before any paperwork: a private call with CF leadership. We map your current situation — license status, production, your WFG hierarchy position — and confirm the move makes sense for you. Nothing is communicated to anyone until you decide.

Book a confidential discovery call.
Bring your questions about compensation, ownership of clients, and independence — this is the meeting for them.
2

Review your WFG agreement

Day 1–5

Your existing agreement may contain non-solicitation or other post-departure obligations. Understanding them before you move is what makes a transition clean instead of contentious.

Locate your signed WFG associate agreement.
Review its non-solicitation and confidentiality clauses.
Escalation point: CF coordinates the transition process, but interpreting your contractual obligations is a legal question. If your agreement contains restrictive clauses, we'll recommend you get independent legal advice — we will never guess at this on your behalf.
3

Verify license & E&O standing

Day 3–7

Your provincial license and continuing education standing transfer with you — no re-exam. We confirm everything is current so nothing surprises us mid-transfer.

Confirm your license is active and CE credits are current.
Confirm your E&O coverage and whether it's individual or provided through your current structure — if it lapses at departure, we arrange replacement coverage before you resign.
4

Prepare CF contracting in parallel

Week 1–2

We prepare your CF Canada Financial contracting package and Apexa updates before your departure date, so your out-of-market gap — the days you can't write new business — is as close to zero as possible.

Complete the CF contracting package and advisor screening checklist.
Update your Apexa profile to reflect the pending transfer (Elena guides the exact sequence and timing).
5

Resign from WFG

Your chosen date

A short, professional, written notice. No drama, no burned bridges — the industry is small and your reputation travels with you.

Submit written notice per your agreement's requirements.
Keep a dated copy of everything you send and receive.
Return any WFG property and complete their exit requirements promptly.
6

Carrier transfers

Week 2–3

Each carrier you're appointed with processes a transfer of your contracting to CF Canada Financial. Some carriers require a release or non-objection from your former organization; others process on notice. We manage each carrier's process for you.

Give Elena your full list of carrier appointments and advisor codes.
Sign the transfer/appointment forms we prepare per carrier.
Track each carrier's confirmation — Elena reports status weekly until all are through.
Good to know: carrier release policies differ and change. Elena confirms the current requirement per carrier for your file rather than relying on what was true last year.
7

Client communication — done compliantly

Week 2–4

What you may say, to whom, and when depends on your former agreement and privacy rules. We help you build a communication plan that protects you while keeping the relationships you've earned.

Build your client communication plan with CF's guidance (and legal advice where clauses require it).
Update your professional profiles — LinkedIn, email signature, voicemail — on the agreed date.
8

Welcome to independence

Week 3–4

Orientation with your CF team: our carrier lineup, advanced-market resources, illustration tools, and the support model. Then the part you came for — writing business as an independent advisor.

Complete CF orientation and platform access setup.
Meet your practice-development contact and set your 90-day plan.
The difference you'll feel: full product shelf across major Canadian carriers, transparent compensation, and no hierarchy between you and your clients.

Confidential by default

Every conversation on this path is private until you decide otherwise. No calls to your office, no traceable communication without your consent.

Plain-language glossary

Captive vs. independent
A captive advisor sells within one organization's structure; an independent advisor contracts through an MGA with access to the full market.
Non-solicitation
A contract clause limiting who you may approach after leaving. Interpretation belongs to a lawyer, not a checklist.
Out-of-market gap
Days between leaving one organization and being contracted at the next, when you can't submit new business. Our job is to minimize it.
Carrier release
Some insurers require your former organization's sign-off before transferring your appointment.

A note on accuracy

Contractual and legal questions about your departure are referred to independent legal advice. Licensing and carrier requirements are confirmed by CF staff for your specific file.

Moving an established practice — without missing a beat.

Coming from PPI, Financial Horizons, IDC WIN, HUB, or another MGA, you don't need onboarding explained — you need a broker-of-record transfer executed precisely, your in-force block handled correctly, and a reason the move was worth it. Here's the sequence.

1

Practice review with CF leadership

Day 1

A peer-level conversation: your carrier mix, in-force block, target market, and what your current MGA isn't giving you. If we're not a better home for your practice, we'll say so.

Book a practice review call with CF leadership.
Outline your carrier appointments, production profile, and any pending cases in the pipeline.
2

Map your current agreements

Day 1–5

Your existing MGA agreement governs how your transfer works — notice requirements, any charge-back or debt provisions, and how in-force compensation is treated on departure.

Review your current MGA agreement's termination and transfer terms.
Confirm whether any advisor debt, advances, or charge-back exposure exists.
Escalation point: how your renewals and trailers move (or don't) is contract-specific and sometimes carrier-specific. CF staff review this with you case by case — and recommend legal advice where the agreement is ambiguous.
3

CF contracting & Apexa update

Week 1

You already have an Apexa profile — this is an update, not a rebuild. Your CF contracting package runs in parallel so both close together.

Sign the CF Canada Financial contracting package and screening checklist.
Update your Apexa profile to reflect CF Canada Financial as your MGA.
Confirm your E&O certificate is current and uploaded.
4

Broker-of-record / AGA transfers per carrier

Week 1–2

Each carrier processes the transfer of your contracting from your current MGA to CF. Requirements vary — some need release letters, some process on notice, and processing times differ by carrier.

Provide your carrier list and advisor codes; Elena prepares each carrier's transfer package.
Sign and return transfer forms as they're prepared.
Receive weekly per-carrier status from Elena until every transfer confirms.
Good to know: pending cases in underwriting need special attention during a transfer — tell Elena about every case in the pipeline so none are orphaned mid-move.
5

In-force block & compensation continuity

Week 2

We confirm servicing rights and compensation flow on your existing block, carrier by carrier, so renewals and service work land where they should from day one at CF.

Reconcile your in-force policy list against carrier confirmations.
Confirm compensation routing with CF's contracting desk.
6

Notify your current MGA

Per your agreement

Professional written notice per your agreement's terms — timed in coordination with the carrier transfers so your out-of-market exposure is minimal.

Submit written notice per your current agreement.
Settle any outstanding items (debt balances, equipment, access) cleanly.
7

Plug into the CF platform

Week 2–3

The reason you moved: advanced-market support for incorporated professionals and business owners — corporate insurance strategies, CDA and passive-income planning, estate freezes — plus practice-development tools most MGAs don't build.

Orientation with CF's advanced-case and practice-development teams.
Access to CF's advisor tools, marketing resources, and carrier-day programs.
Set your 90-day production and pipeline plan with CF leadership.
The milestone: transfers confirmed, block intact, pipeline moving — and a platform behind you that's built for the market you actually serve.

Plain-language glossary

Broker of record
The MGA/agency recognized by a carrier as holding your contract. The transfer changes this designation to CF.
AGA transfer
Associate General Agent transfer — the carrier paperwork moving your contracting between agencies.
In-force block
Your existing policies and the renewal/trailer compensation attached to them.
Charge-back
Commission clawed back when a policy lapses early — relevant when advances exist at your current MGA.

A note on accuracy

Transfer requirements, release policies, and compensation treatment vary by carrier and contract, and they change. Every item on this page is confirmed by CF staff for your specific file before you act on it.
